## Key Findings

- Age is the dominant non-modifiable predictor — every additional decade nearly doubles 10-year CHD odds (OR 1.93, 95% CI 1.70-2.18)
- Among modifiable factors, smoking dose (OR 1.24 per 10 cigs/day), systolic BP (OR 1.14 per 10 mmHg), and glucose (OR 1.07 per 10 mg/dL) are the clearest independent signals
- Diabetes, hypertension, BMI, and cholesterol showed strong crude associations but lost independent strength after adjustment — their signal overlaps with the measured continuous variables
- Risk clustering matters: patients with 3+ risk factors (smoking, hypertension, diabetes, obesity) had 4x the CHD odds vs those with none
- Hypertension + diabetes identified a particularly high-risk subgroup (41.8% incidence vs 10.5% baseline)
