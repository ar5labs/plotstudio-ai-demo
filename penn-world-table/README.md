## Key Findings

- Capital deepening is not well explained by same-year panel regressions — its coefficient is near zero and statistically insignificant across FE, RE, and joint models
- Human-capital growth is the stronger contemporaneous panel correlate, but the association is negative — likely reflecting transitional dynamics where human-capital accumulation occurs during phases of weaker short-run TFP growth
- The Hausman test does not reject random effects, but both FE and RE estimators tell the same story
- In Granger-style dynamic tests, capital deepening shows more frequent short-run predictive precedence (29% of countries) than human capital (17%), suggesting the two drivers operate on different time horizons
- Diagnostics (Breusch-Pagan, Breusch-Godfrey, RESET) all reject, confirming clustered SEs are necessary and the linear specification may be incomplete
